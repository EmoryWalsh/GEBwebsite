The Guiding Eyes for the Blind: Mission

Guiding Eyes for the Blind provides guide dogs to people with vision loss. The organization is passionate about connecting exceptional dogs with individuals for greater independence in their day-to-day lives.

Guiding Eyes has nearly 70 years of experience in providing guide dogs to people with vision loss. It is estimated that more than 10000 service dog teams have graduated since the founding of GEB in 1954. Approximately 100 guide dogs are placed annually, and more than 1700 volunteers support their mission.

Each Guiding Eyes graduate has a unique story of life changing events, challenges, determination, and triumph.

Learn more and donate at https://www.guidingeyes.org/

Guiding Eyes at Cornell: What We Do

  • Work with Guiding Eyes for the Blind, specifically the Fingerlakes Region
  • Host fundraisers to raise money and awareness for Guiding Eyes for the Blind
  • Provide a network of sitters for dogs being raised in our region - prioritizing sitters for our Cornell dogs
  • Raise dogs! Multiple dogs are currently being raised by undergraduate students on campus
